Equipment List

Electric Bass Guitar - You can learn on anything, but for your first bass I recommend investing in an instrument from a reputable brand somewhere in the $150-$300 price range.

Tip - To tell if a brand is any good, go to a music store's website such as guitarcenter.com or your local store's site and check if they carry the brand. Amazon is filled with low quality, knock off brands.

Make sure to start with a 4-string bass and not a 5-string bass. Get a fretted bass and not a fretless bass. Make sure the bass matches your dominant hand (right-handed/left-handed). Long-scale basses are the usual choice, but short-scale basses do have a "thicker" low end sound and may be more comfortable for those with smaller proportions. For a more rock-oriented sound, look into a P-bass pickup configuration. Otherwise, a PJ or J configuration will be more versatile. Also consider a passive vs active bass. Active basses are usually louder and include more tone control knobs. Passive basses are usually a little quieter, but have a warmer, more "natural" sound. This choice will not matter much to you as a beginner. Of course, try in person if you can!

Amplifier - Look for a combo amplifier that combines the electronics and speaker into one box vs the separate head and cab setup. A combo amp is cheaper and has less setup, making it the better choice for a beginner.

The main factor to consider is Wattage - a measure of how loud your amp will be. For home practice, 15-40W will be sufficient. If you plan on playing in a band, 100W may be more appropriate to compete with the drums and guitar. Other nice features to have include overdrive, tuner, active/passive inputs, and tone controls. Head to the Terminology section to learn more about some common features.

Strap - A simple, cheap nylon strap will do the trick.


Ernie Ball 2" Poly Pro Strap - Plenty of colors to choose from

1/4" Cable - Make sure both ends of the cable are 1/4" size. Get a cable at least 10 feet long, but 20 feet is better. You don't need to splurge on gold plated, heavy duty cables, but be ready to spend around $25 so your cable is quality.
