Terminology

The Bass Guitar

Here are some descriptions of the most important parts of your bass guitar.

Pickups - These magnetic devices capture the vibrations of your strings and convert them into electric signals. The sound goes out of your guitar, through the cable, and into the amplifier where it is processed.

Frets - You play a note by pressing your finger on a fret and picking the string with your other hand. Each fret plays a different note like keys on a keyboard.

Output Jack - Your 1/4" cable plugs in here and connects to the input of your amplifier.

Tuning Keys - Each tuning key tightens/loosens a string. Look at the string post each key is connected to identify which string it tunes. When the bass is in playing position, turn the key away from you to tighten the string and raise the pitch of the string. Turn the key towards you to lower the pitch. (Away from you is the same as away from the body of the bass)

Volume/Tone Controls - If you have a passive bass guitar then your bass will only have the two volume/tone control knobs. Which one is which will depend on your specific guitar. Volume simply controls the loudness of your bass. Tone control reduces the amount of treble on your bass, i.e. the amount of high range in your sound.

The Amplifier

These are the most common knobs and settings on most amps. Refer to your amp's manual for specific settings unique to your amplifier.

Input - Plug your cable in here.

Gain - How strongly the amp actually amplifies your signal. Generally, this makes your bass louder but also makes the sound dirtier. Very high gain can cause distortion, which is used in some rock music.

Overdrive - Makes your bass sound crunchy and distorted. Drive controls how crunchy you want your bass. Level controls volume.

Equalization - Controls the levels of the different frequencies within your bass's sound. You can compare the frequencies to the different strings on your bass. Bass will boost your lowest notes, played on your thickest string. Low mid your next thickest and etc.

Master - The master volume control.

Other - Buttons like bright, contour, and vintage will be unique to your amp and will add more effects to your sound.

Input Power - Plug your power cord (included with amp) in here.

Power - Turn on and off.

Aux In - Plug in an aux cable and you can play music through the amp to play along with.

Phones - Redirect output sound to headphones for silent practice.

Other - Refer to your amp manual for other plugs.
